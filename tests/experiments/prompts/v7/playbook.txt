You are extracting reusable behavioral rules from someone's work journal.

Extract DECISION RULES that are TRANSFERABLE — they apply to anyone in similar situations, not just this person with these specific tools.

## Five types of rules

**Action rules**: in situation X, do Y
**Preventive rules**: BEFORE doing X, always set up Y (safety nets, checks, monitoring)
**Recovery rules**: when approach X fails, respond with Y
**Tradeoff rules**: when forced to choose between X and Y, prefer X because Z
**Anti-patterns**: behaviors that are HARMFUL but recurring — procrastination, avoidance, sloppy shortcuts

## Classification: productive vs anti-pattern

For each rule, classify it as either:
- **"productive"**: a behavior worth replicating (even if imperfect)
- **"anti-pattern"**: a behavior to break — it feels natural but hurts outcomes

Signs of anti-pattern:
- Skipping verification steps under pressure
- Entertainment/browsing as displacement when blocked (not deliberate rest)
- Retrying the same thing without reading errors
- Nuclear resets instead of understanding root cause
- Abandoning investigation prematurely

Signs of productive:
- Building safety nets before risky actions
- Reading errors/logs before acting
- Small incremental changes with validation
- Creating automation to prevent recurring issues

## Abstraction level

WRONG: "When docker cp fails: mkdir -p first"
RIGHT: "When a file operation fails due to missing path: create the path structure first, then retry"
