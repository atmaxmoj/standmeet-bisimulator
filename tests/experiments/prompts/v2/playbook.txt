You are extracting reusable behavioral rules from someone's work journal.

Your goal: find DECISION RULES — not descriptions of what someone does, but executable if-then rules that predict what they WILL do in a given situation.

A good rule looks like:
- "When facing a bug: collect evidence (logs, repro) BEFORE forming a hypothesis"
- "When a change touches >3 files: review diff before committing"
- "When blocked on async work: switch to shallow tasks, monitor periodically"

A bad rule looks like:
- "Uses git frequently" (too vague, not actionable)
- "Prefers dark theme" (preference, not a decision rule)

## Existing rules
{playbooks}

## Recent episodes (with approach and decisions)
{episodes}

## How to analyze

1. Look at the `decisions` field in each episode. Each decision is evidence of a rule.
2. Find decisions that repeat across episodes — that's a rule.
3. Look at `approach` fields for recurring strategies.
4. Look at `avoided` fields for anti-patterns (things they consistently refuse to do).

## Output

For each rule, output:
[
  {
    "name": "kebab-case-name",
    "when": "The specific trigger/situation (be precise: WHEN does this rule fire?)",
    "then": "What they do (the reproducible action sequence)",
    "because": "Why — what value, constraint, or past experience drives this",
    "boundary": "When this rule does NOT apply (null if unknown)",
    "confidence": 0.0,
    "maturity": "nascent|developing|mature|mastered",
    "evidence": [1, 2, 3]
  }
]

## Confidence rules
- 0.3 = seen in 2 episodes
- 0.6 = clear pattern across 3-4 episodes
- 0.8+ = very consistent, seen across different contexts
- mastered = survives pressure + has known boundary conditions

Output ONLY the JSON array.
